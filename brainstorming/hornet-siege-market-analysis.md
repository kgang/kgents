# Hornet Siege: Market Viability Analysis

> *"You are the apex predator. You are nature's perfect killing machine. And you will lose."*

**Analysis Date**: 2025-12-28
**Game**: Hornet Siege (formerly WASM Survivors)
**Genre**: Survivors-like / Bullet Heaven Roguelike
**Unique Angle**: Play the villain (Japanese giant hornet). Tragedy narrative. You always lose.

---

## 2. Narrative Innovation: Playing the Villain

### 2.1 Games Where You Play the Monster

| Game | Villain Type | Reception | Lessons |
|------|--------------|-----------|---------|
| **Carrion** | Amorphous horror monster | Highly praised; "reverse-horror power fantasy" | Players love *feeling* powerful |
| **Overlord** | Evil warlord + minions | Cult following; dark humor worked | Humor deflects discomfort |
| **Dungeon Keeper** | Trap-building villain | Classic; spawned genre | Playing evil can be joyful |
| **Untitled Goose Game** | Mischievous goose | 10M+ sales; adorable villain | "Villain" can be charming |
| **Prototype** | Anti-hero with powers | Mixed; moral murkiness hurt | Need clear framing |

**Key Insight**: Players *love* playing villains when the game either:
1. Makes it **fun** (Dungeon Keeper, Untitled Goose Game)
2. Makes it **empowering** (Carrion, Prototype)
3. Makes it **thematically resonant** (Spec Ops: The Line)

**Hornet Siege's Bet**: It's trying for #3 (tragedy) while delivering #2 (predator power fantasy). This is ambitious but valid.

### Additional Villain Game Data (from research agents)

| Game | Sales/Reception | Key Takeaway |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Carrion** | 200K+ first month; BAFTA Best Debut 2021 | Visceral monster gameplay; 94% Steam positive |
| **Dungeon Keeper** | 700K copies by 2003; 93% Steam positive | Whimsical villainy + dark humor |
| **Overlord** | 91% Steam positive; E3 Editor's Choice | Comedy deflects moral discomfort |
| **Loop Hero** | 500K first week; 1M by Dec 2021 | You BUILD your own doom; inverts conventions |

**Critical Pattern**: ~300-375 villain-protagonist games on Steam. Top performers generate up to $19M net revenue. But only when villainy is *fun*, not genuinely disturbing.

**Source**: [The Gamer](https://www.thegamer.com/carrion-horror-games-play-as-villain/), [Eneba Hub](https://www.eneba.com/hub/games/best-games-where-you-are-the-villain/), [Game Rant Carrion Sales](https://gamerant.com/carrion-game-sales/)

### 2.2 Predetermined Loss as Design

| Game | "You Lose" Mechanic | How Players Responded |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Hades** | Death is the loop; you die repeatedly | Embraced; death = story progression |
| **Spec Ops: The Line** | You can't win morally | Divisive but acclaimed; "the avoidable action was playing at all" |
| **Shadow of the Colossus** | You're killing majestic creatures | Tragedy elevated the experience |
| **Roguelikes generally** | Permadeath as feature | "Learning-through-dying rhythm" is satisfying |

**Critical Insight from Hades Analysis**:

> "Supergiant doesn't make games where players can't fail, they make games where players will inevitably fail as a reminder that failure is a crucial part of life. And that's what HADES is about; making failure feel natural in the ebb and flow of existence."
> — [Medium Analysis](https://natalia-nazeem.medium.com/failure-is-death-and-death-is-progress-the-use-of-repetition-replayability-and-narrative-673cfa4e2e8)

**Hornet Siege's Advantage**: Unlike most roguelikes where death feels like failure, Hornet Siege frames death as *thematically appropriate*. You're a predator raiding a superorganism. The superorganism adapts. This is *how nature works*.

**Risk**: If the "you always lose" framing feels *frustrating* rather than *tragic*, the game fails.

### 2.3 The Psychology of Meaningful Failure (Research Agent Findings)

Academic and industry research reveals key principles for making loss feel good:

| Principle | Research Finding | Application to Hornet Siege |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------------|
| **Challenge Orientation** | "Players with high challenge orientation experienced success and failures more similarly" | Target players who enjoy the struggle |
| **Progress Velocity** | "Games stop being enjoyable when expected error reduction velocity is reduced to zero" | Each death must teach something |
| **Positive Discomfort** | Spec Ops study: "fabrication, highlighting of negative consequences, and reversal create complicity that creates powerful experience" | Make the player *feel* responsible |
| **Catharsis Through Choice** | "Unlike books or films, games require players to actively participate, creating stronger emotional connection" | Player agency even in inevitable doom |

**The Roguelike Philosophy**:
> "Whether roguelikes toy with us or kill us quickly, they use death to educate us. They hone us through failure, changing our attitudes about how we play. Games sharpen the mind. Roguelikes simply remind us that being sharpened is, well, sharp."
> — [Den of Geek](https://www.denofgeek.com/games/darkest-dungeon-and-permanent-death-in-video-games/)

**What Makes Loss Meaningful** (from research):
1. **Learning**: Each failure teaches something
2. **Progress**: Meta-progression persists across deaths
3. **Narrative**: Death advances the story (Hades model)
4. **Beauty**: The loss is aesthetically meaningful (Edith Finch)
5. **Agency**: Player had choices, even if doomed (Shadow of the Colossus)
6. **Catharsis**: Emotional release through tragic narrative

**Source**: [Davide Aversa](https://www.davideaversa.it/blog/hades-case-study-storytelling-roguelike-games/), [Sidequest](https://sidequest.zone/2021/11/22/death-is-only-the-beginning-mortality-in-the-roguelike/), [ScienceDirect Failure Research](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1071581923002082), [Game Studies Spec Ops Analysis](https://gamestudies.org/1602/articles/jorgensenkristine)

## 3. The Hornet vs. Bee Phenomenon

### 3.1 Real Biology = Free Marketing

The Japanese giant hornet vs. honey bee "hot defensive bee ball" is:
- **Scientifically fascinating**: Bees raise temperature to 46°C + high CO2, cooking hornets alive
- **Virally popular**: Multiple National Geographic videos, millions of views
- **Emotionally resonant**: Underdogs (bees) defeating apex predators (hornets)
- **Recently newsworthy**: "Murder hornet" coverage in 2020 renewed public interest

**Key Stats**:
- A single hornet can kill **40 bees per minute**
- 30 hornets can destroy **30,000 bees in 4 hours**
- But Japanese honey bees evolved THE BALL defense

**Source**: [Smithsonian Magazine](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/science-nature/bizarre-bee-havior-in-the-battle-against-the-giant-hornet-129395782/), [National Geographic](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/article/120316-hot-bee-balls-hornets-insects-brains-animals-science)

### 3.2 Marketing Angle: "Based on Real Biology"

This is **unique positioning**:

| Typical Survivors-Like | Hornet Siege |
|------------------------|--------------|
| Fantasy monsters | Real insects |
| Arbitrary lore | Documented natural behavior |
| "Cool" aesthetic | "Nature documentary" aesthetic |
| Generic power fantasy | Specific ecological tragedy |

**Potential Hooks**:
- "Play the most infamous predator in the insect world"
- "Based on real biology: Japanese giant hornets vs. honey bee colonies"
- "The bees do the thing from that viral National Geographic video"

**Risk**: Some players won't care about the biology angle. But those who do will become *evangelists*.

## 4. Fun Factor Analysis

### 4.1 Core Loop Assessment

| Element | Fun Potential | Risk |
|---------|---------------|------|
| **Predator movement** | HIGH — Fast, responsive, powerful feeling | Must hit <16ms response |
| **Kill feedback** | HIGH — "Chitin crack + scatter + XP burst" | Must be satisfying |
| **Upgrade system** | HIGH — 8+ abilities, 6 archetypes | Complexity might overwhelm |
| **Bee formations** | MEDIUM-HIGH — Tactical depth | 5 formations may be too many |
| **Colony learning** | MEDIUM — Novel mechanic | Could feel unfair if not balanced |
| **Tragedy narrative** | MEDIUM — Differentiator | May frustrate achievement-focused players |

### 4.2 The "Always Lose" Problem

**Potential Issues**:
1. Players who need to "win" will bounce
2. "Why bother?" nihilism if loss feels inevitable
3. Mastery without victory might feel hollow

**Mitigations (from spec)**:
1. Runs are **short** (~10-15 min) — low investment per loss
2. Mastery is visible — "I survived longer, I killed more"
3. Death is **crystallized** in amber memory — your story is preserved
4. The tragedy framing recontextualizes loss as *narratively appropriate*

**Comparison to Hades**: In Hades, you eventually escape. In Hornet Siege, you never do. This is more extreme. It will polarize.

### 4.3 Complexity Concerns

The spec describes:
- 5 bee types
- 5 defensive formations
- Enhanced bee variants
- Colony learning system
- 8+ hornet abilities
- 6 upgrade archetypes

**Worry**: Is this too complex for a "quick session" survivors-like?

**Counter**: Vampire Survivors is also complex (36+ weapons, evolutions, combos). Depth emerged over time. If the *moment-to-moment* play is simple, complexity can be layered.

**Recommendation**: Start with 2-3 formations. Add others in updates. Let complexity emerge.

## 5. Unique Selling Points

### 5.1 Differentiation Matrix

| Feature | Vampire Survivors | Brotato | Halls of Torment | **Hornet Siege** |
|---------|-------------------|---------|------------------|------------------|
| Theme | Gothic horror | Sci-fi potato | Diablo-esque | **Nature documentary** |
| Narrative | Minimal | Minimal | Minimal | **Tragedy arc** |
| Player role | Survivor/hero | Survivor/hero | Survivor/hero | **Villain/predator** |
| Win condition | Survive 30 min | Clear waves | Survive/progress | **You always lose** |
| Real-world basis | None | None | None | **Real biology** |
| Enemy behavior | Hordes | Waves | Hordes | **Coordinating superorganism** |

**Verdict**: Hornet Siege is the **most differentiated** survivors-like in the market.

### 5.2 Streamability & Clip Moments

| Moment | Clip Potential | Why |
|--------|----------------|-----|
| "THE BALL IS FORMING" | VERY HIGH | Tension, dread, recognizable phrase |
| Colony coordinating | HIGH | Visible AI behavior is fascinating |
| Predator power fantasy | HIGH | Satisfying kills are always shareable |
| Death crystallization | MEDIUM | Reflective, but less action-oriented |

**Comparison to Hades**: Hades is *extremely* streamable. Runs are dramatic, personalities are engaging, deaths are interesting. Hornet Siege has similar potential if the "THE BALL" moment is as dramatic as designed.

### 5.3 Target Audience

**Primary**:
- Roguelike/survivors-like enthusiasts looking for something fresh
- Nature nerds who know the hornet/bee phenomenon
- Players who appreciate thematic depth over power fantasy

**Secondary**:
- Indie game enthusiasts who value originality
- Content creators looking for novel games
- Hades fans who want another "death is the point" experience

**Audience Size Estimate**: Smaller than Vampire Survivors, but more passionate. Think 50K-500K potential players rather than millions.

## 6. Risk Assessment

### 6.1 Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Genre saturation drowns game | HIGH | HIGH | Aggressive differentiation, unique marketing angle |
| "Always lose" frustrates players | MEDIUM | HIGH | Clear framing, short runs, amber memory system |
| Complexity overwhelms | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Phased introduction, start with fewer formations |
| Colony AI is unfair | MEDIUM | HIGH | Extensive playtesting, visible coordination timers |
| Development scope creep | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Stick to spec, cut features if needed |
| Discoverability on Steam | HIGH | MEDIUM | Strong trailer, nature doc hook, streamer outreach |
| Biology angle too niche | LOW | LOW | It's additive, not required for enjoyment |

### 6.2 Financial Projections

**Based on comparable games**:

| Scenario | Unit Sales | Revenue (at $7.99) | Notes |
|----------|------------|-------------------|-------|
| Pessimistic | 10,000 | ~$50K gross | Genre fatigue, poor discoverability |
| Realistic | 50,000-100,000 | ~$400K-$800K gross | Niche hit, passionate audience |
| Optimistic | 250,000+ | ~$2M+ gross | Breakout, viral moment, streamer adoption |

**Break-even Analysis**:
- Solo dev: ~10,000 units covers opportunity cost
- Small team (2-3): ~25,000-50,000 units needed

**Comparison**: Halls of Torment hit 900K players in Early Access. That's the *ceiling* for a well-executed survivors-like in 2024.

### 6.3 Go/No-Go Recommendation

**GO**, with caveats:

1. **Scope**: Start with core loop + 2-3 formations. THE HEAT BALL is mandatory. Others can wait.
2. **Timeline**: Get playable build fast. Test "fun floor" before adding complexity.
3. **Marketing**: Lead with the biology hook. "The bees do the thing."
4. **Expectations**: This is a niche product. Plan for 50K-100K sales, celebrate anything above.

## 8. Recommendations

### 8.1 Immediate Actions

1. **Validate the core loop** — Prototype movement + kills + THE BALL. Does it feel good?
2. **Test "always lose" tolerance** — Playtest with 10+ players. Do they retry or quit?
3. **Create a "THE BALL IS FORMING" clip** — If this moment doesn't land, redesign it

### 8.2 Marketing Strategy

1. **Lead with biology** — Partner with nature/science accounts on TikTok
2. **Trailer hook**: Show the real-world phenomenon, then show the game
3. **Streamer outreach**: This is a "react" game. Content creators will have fun with THE BALL

### 8.3 Development Priorities

| Priority | Feature | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| 1 | Core movement + kills | Fun floor |
| 2 | THE HEAT BALL | Signature moment |
| 3 | 2-3 abilities | Build identity |
| 4 | Colony coordination visual | Readable doom |
| 5 | Death crystallization | Narrative closure |
| Later | Other formations | Add depth post-launch |

### 8.4 What Would Kill This Project

1. **Movement that doesn't feel predatory** — The hornet fantasy is speed + power
2. **THE BALL that doesn't feel inevitable** — The doom must be readable
3. **Deaths that feel unfair** — Every death must be attributable
4. **Complexity that obscures the core** — Simplify before adding

---

## Conclusion

Hornet Siege is a **high-risk, high-differentiation** project in a crowded genre. Its strength is its *opinion*: you are the monster, you lose, and that's the point.

The survivors-like market is saturated, but players are hungry for fresh takes. The hornet/bee biology angle is genuinely novel. The tragedy narrative—if executed well—will create cult status.

**Final Verdict**: Build it. But build the *smallest thing that captures the tragedy* first. If THE BALL doesn't make players gasp, nothing else matters.
