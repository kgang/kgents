## Key Insight: Memory is Theatre, Not Archive

The profound realization from exploring M-gents and N-gents together:

**Memory is not a filing cabinet. It's a theatre company.**

- The **Historian** collects props (crystals)
- The **Memory** arranges the stage (HoloMap)
- The **Bard** performs the play (narrative)
- The **Echo Chamber** is rehearsal (replay)
- **Forgetting** is editing the script
- **Compression** is blocking the scenes
- **Landmarks** are signature moments
- **Desire lines** are choreography

When you ask for a memory, you're not retrieving a file - you're **staging a performance**. The quality depends on:
- Available props (crystals)
- Stage arrangement (memory topology)
- Director's vision (genre/perspective)
- Actor's skill (Bard quality)
- Budget (tokens)

This explains why:
- Same cue → different recall (different staging)
- Forgetting isn't loss (editing improves the show)
- Resolution varies (close-up vs wide shot)
- Stories change (different directors, same material)

**The UX implication**: Don't show "loading memory" - show "staging memory". Don't say "compressed" - say "simplified for budget". Don't say "forgot" - say "let go to make room".
